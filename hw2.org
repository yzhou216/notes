* Problem 1.

Let $\mathcal{R}$ be the region bounded by the graphs of $y = e^x$, $y
= e^{-x}$ and the line $x = 1$.

** (a)

Find the volume of the solid obtained by rotating $\mathcal{R}$ about
the y-axis.

#+begin_export latex
\begin{center}

#+end_export

Write equations as functions of $y$.

#+begin_export latex
\begin{align}
y &= e^x \\
\text{Let } f(y) = x &= \ln{y} \\
y &= e^{-x} \\
\text{Let } g(y) = x &= -\ln{y} \\
\text{Let } h(y) = x &= 1
\end{align}
#+end_export

Find all intersections of $f(y)$, $g(y)$ and $h(y)$.

#+begin_export latex
\begin{align}
f(y) &= h(y) \\
\ln{y} &= 1 \\
y &= e
\end{align}
#+end_export

$h(y)$ and $f(y)$ intersects at $y = e$.

#+begin_export latex
\begin{align}
g(y) &= h(y) \\
-\ln{y} &= 1 \\
y &= \frac{1}{e}
\end{align}
#+end_export

$h(y)$ and $g(y)$ intersects at $y = \frac{1}{e}$.

#+begin_export latex
\begin{align}
V_\mathcal{R}
&= \int_\frac{1}{e}^e{\pi \cdot (h(y))^2}\, dy - \int_\frac{1}{e}^1{\pi \cdot (g(y))^2}\, dy - \int_1^e{\pi \cdot (f(y))^2}\, dy  \\
&= \pi \cdot \left[\int_\frac{1}{e}^e{h^2(y)}\, dy - \left(\int_\frac{1}{e}^1{g^2(y)}\, dy + \int_1^e{f^2(y)}\, dy \right)\right] \\
&= \pi \cdot \left[\int_\frac{1}{e}^e{1}\, dy - \left(\int_\frac{1}{e}^1{(-\ln(y))^2}\, dy + \int_1^e{\ln^2(y)}\, dy \right)\right] \\
&= \pi \cdot \left[y\Big|_\frac{1}{e}^e - \left((y \ln^2{y} - 2 y \ln{y} + 2y)\Big|_1^e + (y \ln^2{y} - 2y \ln{y} + 2y)\Big|_\frac{1}{e}^1 \right)\right] \\
&= \pi \cdot \left[y\Big|_\frac{1}{e}^e - (y\, \ln^2{y} - 2y\, \ln{y} + 2y)\Big|_\frac{1}{e}^e \right] \\
&= \pi \cdot \left[(e - \frac{1}{e}) - \left((e\ln^2{e} - 2e \ln{e} + 2e) - (\frac{1}{e}\ln^2{\frac{1}{e}} - 2 \cdot \frac{1}{e} \ln{\frac{1}{e}} + 2 \cdot \frac{1}{e})\right)\right] \\
&= \frac{4\pi}{e}
\end{align}
#+end_export
